### "कोशेली घर" (Koseli Ghar / souvenir house)

- **Status:** Active call for proposals (`प्रस्ताव आह्वान`). The notice is currently pinned to the homepage ticker on every page.
- **What the corpus says:** The municipality is calling for proposals to **operate** a Koseli Ghar (literally "gift/souvenir house"). Three crawled file IDs land on this notice: `2a/2a3187cd653d2dc9.txt`, `4a/4ac5a229db32aa4b.txt`, `80/80806c31ee7748f2.txt` — but each contains only the title; the operational details live in a linked PDF that the text-extraction did not include.
- **Implication for our deck:** This is the single most direct alignment between the municipality's announced agenda and our farmer-to-consumer marketplace pitch. The municipality is, *right now*, soliciting an operator for a physical retail/souvenir outlet. Their conception of "what should sell at Jiri" is precisely organic produce + local handicrafts (kiwi, akbare khursani, yak cheese, churpi, padamchaal-infused liquor, shilma, rilduk — all named on the city profile). This is a natural framing partner for our pitch.

## 4. Strategic context

### Post-2015 earthquake reconstruction (private housing)

- The municipality has a permanent **`निजी आवास पुननिर्माण शाखा`** (Private Housing Reconstruction Branch) prominently in the right-rail of every page — **this is treated as a current, ongoing concern**, not a closed chapter.
- Two persistent items in the right-rail:
  1. **`गुनासो बाट सम्बोधन भएको लभग्राही र पहिला पहिचान भएको लभग्राही`** ("Beneficiaries addressed via grievance + originally-identified beneficiaries") — i.e. the municipality publishes per-ward grievance-resolved beneficiary rosters for wards 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 (separately downloadable per ward).
  2. **`आवास पुननिर्माण तथा प्रवलिकरण सम्बन्धी नगरपालिकाको प्रोफाइल`** ("Municipality profile related to housing reconstruction and retrofitting") — published in both Nepali and English.
- Reconstruction is positioned as significant unfinished work — not a victory lap.

## 6. Anything surprising / non-obvious

These are the items that sharpen our pitch framing or suggest we adjust it.

1. **The municipality is *already* trying to operate a marketplace/retail outlet.** The **Koseli Ghar** call for proposals (currently pinned, top-of-every-page) means we are not pitching into a vacuum. There is an active operator-search for what is essentially a souvenir/local-produce retail outlet. We can position our platform as the **digital + logistics layer** that makes a Koseli Ghar physically operable, profitable, and discoverable to incoming tourists — rather than competing with the concept.

2. **The municipality has *already* funded packaging and branding.** The "rare/heirloom-crop packaging and branding material" call (`रैथाने बालीमा आधारित उत्पादनको प्याकेजिङ तथा ब्रान्डिडमा प्रयोग हुने सामग्री सहयोगको प्रस्ताव आह्वान`) means producers are already being equipped to make consumer-ready packaged products. Our marketplace fits the *next* layer: getting those packaged products to consumers (in-bazaar tourists, urban consumers via delivery riders, B2B restaurant buyers).

3. **Visit Year 2025 alignment is real and explicit.** A dedicated `भ्रमण वर्ष २०२५` (Visit Year 2025) navigation section exists. Our deck for an April 2026 audience can position the platform as the **lasting digital infrastructure** that captures the Visit-Year tourist surge into recurring economic value — answering the implicit "what happens after the tourist year ends?" question.

4. **The agriculture branch is staffed thinly.** The only crawled agriculture-branch staffer (श्रेया आर्याल, Agriculture Graduate Technician) is currently flagged "former employee" on her profile. Either the post is vacant or staff turnover has been recent. This is a credibility argument for an external technology partner: the municipality has program ambition (subsidies, mini-tillers, beekeeping, soil testing, seed potato, organic fertilizer) outpacing internal agriculture-extension staff capacity.

5. **The mayor and ward chairs are overwhelmingly Jirel-ethnicity surnames.** The city profile names Jirels as the indigenous group of the area; six of the nine ward chairs and the mayor himself are Jirel. The deck should honor Jirel-led governance and ethnic identity (not generic "Nepali municipality" framing). The profile also names Brahmin, Chhetri, Sunuwar, Sherpa, Tamang, Newar, and Dalit communities as resident — heterogeneity matters.

6. **Earthquake reconstruction is *still* foregrounded after 11 years.** The "Private Housing Reconstruction Branch" remains the first item in the every-page right-rail, with per-ward grievance/beneficiary rosters still being published. This means: do **not** open with "post-disaster recovery is over, now you can grow." Instead, frame the platform as the income-side complement to ongoing physical rebuild — getting cash flowing to households whose homes are still being completed.

7. **Ward 1 chair has two different phone numbers in the corpus.** Individual profile says 9851000671; consolidated roster says 9849307771. Worth verifying with the ward office (which is listed at ९८५११२५६५५) before any direct outreach.

8. **The 2078 Census shows a female-majority population (51.5%).** Combined with 2.7% disability rate and a 16-percentage-point literacy gap by gender, the platform's **rider/consumer/farmer onboarding** should explicitly handle Devanagari-only users and OTP-only authentication for low-literacy female farmers — which our existing phone-OTP + bilingual UI already does. Worth surfacing in the deck.

9. **Jiri sits at an extraordinary altitude band (1,649 m – 5,341 m within a single municipality).** This means our delivery-fee model and rider-trip routing must honor terrain, not just OSRM road distance. Jata Pokhari (4,229 m) and Panch Pokhari (4,501 m) are inside the municipality boundary. Tourist destinations at those altitudes are not deliverable by motorcycle riders; they are seasonal trek destinations that may *generate* demand for produce delivered at lower-elevation trailheads.

10. **There is a government cattle genetic resource center in Jiri.** The city profile claims it is "the only such center in Nepal" (`नेपालकै एक मात्र सरकारी गाई आनुवांसिक श्रोत केन्द्र`). For a livestock/dairy angle of our platform, this is a uniquely citable institutional anchor.

11. **The native crops list is unusually rich and brandable.** Beyond kiwi/potato/yak-cheese/churpi, the profile names indigenous foodways: **पदमचाल जडिबुटी राखिएको स्थानीय मदिरा** (local liquor infused with the herb padamchaal), **शिल्मा** (shilma — a fermented food), **रिल्दुक** ("the indigenous potato dish"). Any deck mockup of our marketplace listings should use these exact named SKUs.
