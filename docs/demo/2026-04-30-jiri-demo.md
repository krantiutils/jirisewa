## 1. Executive summary / कार्यकारी सारांश

**English.** JiriSewa is a digital platform that connects Jiri's farmers
directly with consumers in Kathmandu through community riders, with a
network of pickup hubs to consolidate produce and shorten the road to
market. The mobile and web apps are working today, support both Nepali
and English, and have a verified end-to-end flow from farmer to home.
The first physical asset we are asking the Jiri municipality to host is a
**Pickup Hub at the Jiri bazaar** — a single roofed drop-off point that
becomes the daily aggregation node for every farmer in the ward. In
return, the municipality gets monthly tonnage reports, farmer welfare
metrics, and a "Jiri-grown" certification it can put its name on.

**नेपाली.** जिरीसेवा एउटा डिजिटल प्लेटफर्म हो जसले जिरीका
किसानहरूलाई सामुदायिक राइडरमार्फत सिधै काठमाडौँका उपभोक्तासँग जोड्छ —

## 2. The problem we are solving

For a household in Jiri, the path from harvest to a Kathmandu kitchen is
broken in three places:

1. **The farm-to-bazaar gap.** Most small farmers cannot leave their farm
   to wait for a buyer. Produce stays on the field and rots, or it sells
   at half price to whoever happens to drive by.
2. **The bazaar-to-Kathmandu gap.** A single household's harvest is too
   small for a truck operator to bother with, but too perishable to wait
   for next week. There is no daily aggregation point that consolidates
   small farmers into a truckload.
3. **The Kathmandu last-mile gap.** Even when produce reaches Kalimati,
   getting it to a household door in Bhaktapur or Boudha is a separate
   problem.

JiriSewa is solving these three gaps in three phases. Phase 1 — the
**aggregation hub at Jiri bazaar** — is built and running today, and is
the part where the Jiri municipality can directly help.

## 4. Phase 1 — Aggregation hubs (live today)

This is the new feature we built in this sprint and the one most relevant
to the Jiri municipality. A **pickup hub** is a managed drop-off point
where farmers leave their produce; the hub operator confirms receipt;
and a rider (or, in Phase 4, a scheduled truck) picks it up. It moves
the platform from "rider waits at every farmer's gate" to "rider stops
once at the hub" — the unlock for tonnage and for farmers who can't run
their own deliveries.

We have seeded the **Jiri Bazaar Hub** as the first hub on the platform.
Every screenshot in this section is real; every action shown is exercised
by automated tests on every commit.

### 4.1 The admin assigns a hub and an operator

![Admin → Hubs — list of all pickup hubs with municipality, operator, type, and active status](screenshots/web/20-admin-hubs.png)
*Admin → Hubs. Lists every hub. The seeded Jiri Bazaar Hub shows with its operator, municipality (Jiri), type (origin), and active status.*

![Admin → New Hub — create a hub with name in both languages, address, GPS, hub type, and operator assignment](screenshots/web/21-admin-hubs-new.png)
*Admin → New Hub. Hubs are created with name in both languages, address, GPS, type (origin / destination / transit — origin = Jiri side), and the operator who will run it.*

### 4.2 The farmer drops off at the hub

![Farmer → Drop off at hub — pick the hub, pick a listing, enter quantity, get a printable lot code](screenshots/web/14-farmer-hubs-dropoff.png)
*Farmer → Drop off at hub. The form shows the farmer's existing listings; on submit the system generates a **lot code** (e.g. NYGJ38) printed on the bag.*

![Drop-off success — the lot code and recent dropoff history showing in_inventory status](screenshots/web/15-farmer-hubs-success.png)
*The dropoff is recorded with an expiry (default 48 hours); the farmer's listing automatically flips to "available at hub" so consumers can place orders that source from the hub instead of the farm.*

### 4.3 The hub operator manages inventory

![Hub operator inventory — all dropoffs at the operator's hub with status filter tabs and per-row actions](screenshots/web/30-hub-inventory.png)
*Hub operator dashboard. Every drop-off at this hub, filterable by status. The operator clicks "Mark received" when they physically take possession; "Spoiled" if produce arrived bad.*

![Awaiting receipt — the queue of dropoffs the farmer recorded, waiting for the operator to confirm](screenshots/web/31-hub-inventory-dropped-off.png)
*"Awaiting receipt" tab. The operator reads the lot code off the bag and confirms — flips status to in_inventory and notifies the farmer.*

![In inventory — produce received and awaiting rider pickup; one-tap dispatch when the rider arrives](screenshots/web/32-hub-inventory-in-inventory.png)
*"In inventory" tab. Produce is on the hub's shelf, waiting to be picked up by a rider or scheduled truck.*

### 4.4 What this unlocks, in plain language

- A farmer with five kilos of curd no longer waits at the gate. They
  drop the bag at the bazaar, get a paper slip with a lot code, and walk
  back to the field. The operator confirms receipt; the rider picks up
  on their next trip.
- Five small farmers now look like one 25-kilo lot to the rider —
  meaning more small farmers fit one trip, and over time, one truck.
- Every farmer-side action lands in the **notifications inbox** (see
  below) so households without smartphones still get an SMS when their
  produce is received and again when it ships.

## 6. The roadmap — five phases

Phase 1 (hubs) is shipped. The remaining four phases are the path to
real volume and KTM coverage.

| Phase | What it adds | Why it matters | Status |
|-------|--------------|----------------|--------|
| 1 | **Aggregation hubs** (origin-side) | Physical drop-off point at Jiri bazaar; tonnage starts to consolidate | **Live** ✅ |
| 2 | **Subscription deliveries** | Weekly produce box; smooths supply, locks in revenue | Schema done, worker pending (~1 week) |
| 3 | **Quality grading + dispute policy** | Pickup/delivery photos, A/B/C grades, 24h refund window | Planned (~1 week) |
| 4 | **Truck-route domain** | Scheduled trucks Jiri↔Kathmandu, multi-leg fulfillment | Planned (~3 weeks) |
| 5 | **KTM destination hubs** | 4–5 city-side hubs (Kalimati, Boudha, Jawalakhel, Kapan, Bhaktapur) for last-mile | Planned (~2 weeks after Phase 4) |

The full plan with schema deltas, RPCs, exit criteria, and risks is at
[`docs/plans/2026-04-29-phased-buildout-plan.md`](../plans/2026-04-29-phased-buildout-plan.md).

## 7. The ask — what we need from Jiri Nagarpalika / जिरी नगरपालिकाबाट हाम्रो अनुरोध

### English

We are not asking the municipality to run logistics. We are asking it to
**lend its weight to the things only the municipality can lend weight
to.** Specifically:

1. **A physical aggregation point at Jiri bazaar.** A roofed area with a
   table and a cabinet — not a building project, an existing space. We
   provide the operator, the system, the lot codes, the rider network.
   The municipality provides the address.
2. **A public endorsement.** A signed memorandum that names the
   municipality as a partner in farmer welfare and rural-economic-data
   infrastructure.
3. **A "Jiri-grown" certification.** A name and a logo the municipality
   lends to verified Jiri produce. We do the QA and the platform; the
   municipality lends the name. No cost to the municipality.
4. **Optional: a 3-month fuel subsidy.** A bootstrap subsidy on the
   first scheduled truck route Jiri↔Kathmandu, repaid out of platform
   revenue once we cross a tonnage threshold. Not required, but
   meaningful.

### In return, the municipality gets

- **Monthly tonnage reports** by crop, by ward, by farmer cohort.
- **Farmer welfare metrics:** number of farmers earning every month,
  average revenue per farmer-household, count of newly verified
  farmers, count of disputes resolved in their favour.
- **A "Jiri-grown" brand** that travels to Kathmandu kitchens with the
  municipality's name on it.
- **A dashboard view** scoped to Jiri ward data, available to ward
  officers in real time.

### नेपाली

हामी नगरपालिकालाई "ढुवानी सञ्चालन गर्न" अनुरोध गरिरहेका छैनौँ।
हामी नगरपालिकालाई **त्यस काममा सहयोग गर्न अनुरोध गरिरहेका छौँ जुन
काम नगरपालिकाले मात्र गर्न सक्छ।** ठोस आवश्यकताहरू यी हुन्:

1. **जिरी बजारमा एउटा भौतिक सङ्ग्रह स्थान।** छानामुनिको खुला ठाउँ —
   एउटा टेबल र एउटा दराज भए पुग्छ। यो नयाँ निर्माण होइन; अहिले
   उपलब्ध रहेको ठाउँ नै हुनुपर्छ। सञ्चालक, सिस्टम, लट कोड, राइडर —
   यी सबै कुरा हामी व्यवस्थापन गर्दछौँ। नगरपालिकाले ठाउँ मात्र
   उपलब्ध गराउनु पर्दछ।
2. **एउटा सार्वजनिक समर्थन।** किसान कल्याण र ग्रामीण आर्थिक तथ्याङ्क
   पूर्वाधारको साझेदारका रूपमा नगरपालिकाको नाम राखिएको हस्ताक्षरित
   समझदारी–पत्र (MoU)।
3. **"जिरी-उत्पादित" प्रमाणीकरण।** नगरपालिकाले आफ्नो नाम र लोगो
   प्रमाणित जिरी उत्पादनसँग जोड्न दिनुहोस्। गुणस्तर परीक्षण र
   प्लेटफर्मको सञ्चालन हामी गर्दछौँ; नगरपालिकाले केवल आफ्नो नाम
   प्रदान गरे पुग्छ। नगरपालिकालाई कुनै आर्थिक भार पर्दैन।
4. **वैकल्पिक: ३ महिनाको इन्धन अनुदान।** जिरी–काठमाडौँ पहिलो
   तालिकाबद्ध ट्रक मार्गका लागि सुरुवाती चरणको अनुदान; तौलको निश्चित
   सीमा पार गरेपछि प्लेटफर्मको आम्दानीबाट फिर्ता गरिनेछ। अनिवार्य
   होइन, तर भयो भने ठूलो टेवा पुग्छ।

### बदलामा नगरपालिकालाई के पाइन्छ

- **उत्पादनको मासिक तौल रिपोर्ट** — बालीअनुसार, वडाअनुसार, किसान
  समूह अनुसार।
- **किसान कल्याणका तथ्याङ्क:** हरेक महिना आम्दानी गर्ने किसानको
  सङ्ख्या, प्रति किसान-घरधुरी औसत आम्दानी, नयाँ प्रमाणित किसानको
  सङ्ख्या, समाधान भएका विवादहरूको सङ्ख्या।
- **"जिरी-उत्पादित" ब्रान्ड** — काठमाडौँका भान्साहरूसम्म
  नगरपालिकाको नाम बोकेर पुग्ने।
- **जिरी वडाको डेटाका लागि छुट्टै ड्यासबोर्ड** — वडा अधिकारीहरूलाई
  वास्तविक समयमा उपलब्ध।

## 8. What tomorrow's demo will look like

A 12–15 minute live walkthrough on a laptop and a phone, in this order:

1. **2 min — open the consumer marketplace** in Nepali. Show 4–5 real
   produce listings from a real Jiri farmer. Click into one, show the
   farmer profile.
2. **2 min — switch to the farmer dashboard** as the same Jiri farmer.
   Show their listings, pending orders, and earnings.
3. **3 min — drop off at the hub.** From the farmer dashboard, click
   "Drop off at hub", select Jiri Bazaar, choose a listing, enter 5 kg.
   Show the lot code that appears.
4. **2 min — switch to the hub operator account.** Open the operator
   dashboard. The drop-off the farmer just made appears in the
   "Awaiting" tab. Click "Mark received". Watch the row move to "In
   inventory" and a notification fire to the farmer.
5. **2 min — show the rider phone.** Open the rider home tab on the
   Android phone. The trip card shows the Jiri→Kathmandu run with
   capacity. Show the available-orders surface where pings appear.
6. **2 min — admin tour.** Show the admin dashboard, hubs page, users
   page — emphasising that the Jiri ward dashboard view is available to
   the municipality as a separate scoped view.
7. **2 min — the ask.** Walk through section 7 above with the deck.
